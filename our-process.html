Our Process

Every engagement follows the same four-phase framework: Discover, Architect, Implement, and Maintain. This structure isn't just a tagline — it's the reason our projects finish on time, on scope, and with documentation your team can actually use. We don't skip steps, and we don't hand things off without making sure you're ready to own what we built together.

The Four Phases

Each phase has a defined set of activities, outputs, and checkpoints. You'll always know where we are, what we're working on, and what comes next.

1

Discover

We invest real time at the start to understand your organization — not just the technology, but the people, workflows, and cultural context that shape how your IT environment needs to function. The decisions we make in this phase inform everything that follows.

What We Do

  • Stakeholder interviews with IT, HR, security, and operations leadership
  • Current-state platform audit — configurations, policies, enrollment profiles, and coverage gaps
  • Device inventory and fleet composition review (Windows, macOS, iOS, Android, and shared devices)
  • Workflow mapping — onboarding, offboarding, provisioning, and support escalation paths
  • Identity infrastructure review — directory services, SSO, MFA, and access governance
  • Security posture baseline assessment against applicable frameworks

What You Receive

  • Current-state summary document with key findings and risk areas
  • Stakeholder alignment memo — confirming goals, constraints, and success criteria
  • Gap list prioritized by risk level, with supporting evidence
  • Engagement scope confirmation based on discovery findings
2

Architect

With a clear picture of where you are, we design where you need to go. The architecture phase produces a detailed technical blueprint — not a slide deck with high-level boxes and arrows, but a documented plan your team can review, question, and approve before a single change is made.

What We Do

  • UEM platform architecture design — tenant structure, group policy hierarchy, and enrollment flows
  • Security framework mapping — Zero Trust design, conditional access logic, and compliance policy structure
  • Identity integration design — directory sync, SSO configuration, MFA enforcement plan
  • Application management strategy — required, optional, and LOB app deployment approach
  • Migration or rollout sequencing — phased approach with rollback procedures defined
  • Documentation framework design — naming conventions, change log structure, and runbook templates

What You Receive

  • Technical architecture document with rationale for every major design decision
  • Phased implementation roadmap with milestones and dependencies
  • Risk register for the proposed architecture with mitigation notes
  • Sign-off checkpoint — no implementation begins until you've reviewed and approved the plan
3

Implement

Implementation is where the architecture becomes reality. We execute methodically — with a pilot phase before broad rollout, documented testing at each milestone, and minimal disruption to your users as the guiding constraint throughout.

What We Do

  • Platform configuration — enrollment profiles, device policies, compliance rules, and application deployment
  • Identity integration — directory sync, SSO federation, MFA rollout, and conditional access policies
  • Security baseline deployment — encryption enforcement, patch compliance, DLP, and threat detection integration
  • Pilot group testing — controlled rollout to a representative test group with issue tracking and iteration
  • Broad rollout coordination — phased device enrollment with user communication and help desk preparation
  • Change log maintenance — every configuration change documented in real time throughout implementation

What You Receive

  • Fully configured and tested UEM environment aligned to the approved architecture
  • Pilot test results and issue resolution log
  • Rollout plan with communication templates for IT and end users
  • Implementation change log — a complete record of every change made and why
4

Maintain

An endpoint environment isn't a project — it's an operational system that evolves with your organization, your threat landscape, and your vendor platforms. The Maintain phase keeps your environment healthy, documented, and aligned long after the initial build is complete.

What We Do

  • Ongoing policy management — routine updates, new policy requirements, and exception handling
  • OS and application update coordination — testing, staging, and deployment with minimal disruption
  • Compliance monitoring — proactive identification of non-compliant or unmanaged devices
  • Configuration drift correction — catching and reversing unauthorized or accidental changes
  • Quarterly environment reviews — health check, metrics review, and forward-looking recommendations
  • Documentation refresh — keeping architecture docs, SOPs, and runbooks current with every change

What You Receive

  • Monthly platform health and compliance reports
  • Quarterly review summaries with metrics trends and recommendations
  • Always-current documentation — architecture docs, change logs, and runbooks
  • Proactive alerts for emerging issues before they become incidents

What Makes Our Process Different

Process is easy to describe. The hard part is actually following it when the pressure is on to move faster or skip steps. Here's where we hold the line.

We Listen Before We Build

Most consulting engagements fail not because of bad execution, but because of bad assumptions made before the work started. We don't show up with a predetermined solution. The Discover phase is non-negotiable — every recommendation we make is grounded in what we actually learned about your environment, your team, and your goals.

Documentation Is Part of the Work

We document as we go — not as an afterthought at project close. Every configuration decision, policy rationale, and architectural choice is captured in real time. By the time we hand off the environment, you have a complete, current record of how it was built and why — not a promise to write something up later.

You Own the Knowledge

We build for handoff, not dependency. Knowledge transfer isn't a separate line item — it's embedded into every phase of the engagement. Your team receives training, documented SOPs, and direct access to us for questions during the support period so they're equipped to operate the environment confidently on their own.

We Design for Where You're Going

Every architecture recommendation accounts for your organization's direction — anticipated headcount growth, platform roadmap changes, evolving compliance requirements, and the operational capacity of your IT team. We don't build to solve today's problem in a way that creates tomorrow's.

Transparent Communication Throughout

You'll never have to chase us for a status update. We establish communication cadence at the start of every engagement — regular check-ins, milestone reviews, and a shared tracking document so you always know what's been done, what's in progress, and what's coming next.

No Vendor Bias, No Shortcuts

Our recommendations are based on what's right for your environment — not what's easiest for us to deploy, what a vendor is paying us to promote, or what we most recently implemented elsewhere. If the best answer is a platform you already own, we'll tell you that. If it requires a difficult migration, we'll tell you that too.

What a Typical Engagement Looks Like

Every project is scoped individually, but here's a general sense of how engagements typically unfold from first conversation to completion.

Week 1

Discovery & Kickoff

Stakeholder interviews, environment audit, and engagement scope confirmation.

Weeks 2–3

Architecture & Design

Technical design documentation, roadmap development, and stakeholder review and sign-off.

Weeks 4–8

Implementation

Platform configuration, pilot group testing, issue resolution, and phased rollout to the full environment.

Week 9

Handoff & Enablement

Admin training, documentation delivery, runbook walkthrough, and knowledge transfer sessions.

Ongoing

Managed Support (Optional)

Transition to managed operations for clients who want continued platform support and monitoring.

Complex migrations, multi-platform environments, or large enterprise deployments may extend the timeline. We'll give you a realistic scope estimate after the initial discovery conversation — never a number pulled from a rate card.
